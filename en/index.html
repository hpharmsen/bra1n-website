No shortage of AI tools.
A shortage of clarity.

The market is full of vendors with big promises. Your organization sees dozens of possible applications, but where do you start? Many AI initiatives start with technology instead of the business — and stall for that reason.

The questions every management team asks:

  • Where does AI actually create value for us, and where doesn't it?
  • What is the business case?
  • Which technology is mature enough, and what is mostly marketing?
  • How do we manage the risks?
  • How do we make it stick in the organization?

We help you answer these questions. No hype, no sales pitch.

Structure, clarity and realism
in the AI discussion

We advise management teams of mid-sized organizations on AI: where to invest, where to wait and where to stay away. Our advice is independent — we don't sell tools and have no stake in vendors.

We start with strategy, processes and numbers, not technology. And we don't stop at a report: we translate opportunities into priorities, a business case and working solutions.

Starting point

From confusion to priorities in four hours

An on-site workshop with your management team. We prepare for your company and industry; you get an honest picture of what AI can mean for your organization.

You go home with:

  • An opportunity map of your organization
  • Prioritized opportunities, assessed on value, feasibility and risk
  • Quick wins you can start with tomorrow
  • Risks and preconditions
  • A proposal for next steps

The goal is not to write an AI strategy. The goal is for your management team to decide where AI deserves attention, money and action. "Do nothing for now" is also a valid outcome — if it's well-founded.
